What is Valorant?

Valorant is a free-to-play first-person hero shooter developed and published by Riot Games, for Microsoft Windows.

How to Play Valorant?

Once you have properly made a RIOT account and downloaded the game, you can finally start playing.

This 5v5 firt-person-shooter game offers a very similar play style to the other popular FPS game CSGO.

Before you enter your first round, you get to pick from a group of Agents, each with their own role and special skills designed to work alongside each other toward a win (see agents list later on the page).

After everyone selects their Agent (even if you picked a bad one), you’ll jump into a random map (see list of maps later on the page) and formally enter the first round.

Start a round as either attacker or defender and switch sides in between. Before you do either, you’ll need to navigate the buy phase, which lets you purchase weapons and abilities. What you buy shapes how you can win.

Now that you have seen all of the gun options, its time to decide what to buy on the first round. Depending on your agent, you will want to buy what fits your play style and amount of money.

At the start of the first round you start with 800 credits (in game money) this only allows you to buy pistols. Depending on the amount of roudns you win, the amount of kills you get and how much you spent the past rounds, will decide your money for that round.

Understanding what to buy, such as abilities or guns, can determine how successful you are at Valorant.

To win the round as attackers, you must either kill the entire oppsoing team, or plant the spike(a bomb) and wait for it to detonate. Some maps may be attacker or defender sided.

To win on defense, you must either kill the entire opposing team, wait for the time in the round to run out (meaning the attackers ran out of time to plant the spike), or defuse the spike once it is planted.

Technically, there is no right or wrong way to win or lose a round, but you will get made fun of for winning or losing a round in specific ways :)

Agent Roles

Duelist

Duelists. Duelists are “self-sufficient fraggers,” according to Riot's official description. They're the agents that create some of the most impact, offering aggression to a team comp. A Duelist should be expected to seek out engagements and frag, may that be finding the opening pick or clutching out a round.

Initiator

The abilities of agents under the Initiator role focus on flushing enemies out of hiding spots and corners in order to assist the team when charging into battle.

Sentinel

These agents are “defensive experts who can lock down areas and watch flanks, both on attack and defender rounds.” Since the game involves planting or defusing the Spike, Sentinels are very important.

Controller

The abilities of agents under the Controller role allow them to assist their teammates by cutting enemy vision, taking control of an area, and forcing enemies into choke points.
